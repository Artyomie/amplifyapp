Chickpea Curry

The term curry has no specific definition, it can mean anything from a stew or flavor soup of Asian origin, a powdered herb blend, an aromatic plant, and more. To me though, it whatever form you experience “curry”, it means to savor the pure essence of delicious, to partake in flavor explosions and combinations beyond comprehensible for the human brain. I invite you to embark on a magical curry journey, to explore a roller coaster of emotions and a journey of flavors. This specific version of curry “Channa Masala” or Chickpea Curry, we will embark our curry journey to India. By channeling traditional flavor profiles of the many different Indian cuisines. We will have aromatics like garlic, ginger, and onions marry with classic spices like garam masala, turmeric, black pepper to create our “essence” of curry flavors. Then as we simmer on the chickpeas, tomatoes, and coconut milk it will take the most commonly known form of curry, a stew. This journey will ultimately be finished off by adding in our last but certainly not least, flavor components (cilantro and lime). In order to balance the powerful and exceedingly magical time of what it is to enjoy curry, bring a friend by its side to balance the journey (lime rice, naan, yogurt, roti, or potatoes) are all acceptable curry friends that will ultimately give you the most harmonious Curry experience ever. I hope you enjoy, if you do please support our website by sharing with your friends or making a purchase from our store, or leaving a review! Thank you.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3/4 inch ginger, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 2 (14.5 oz) cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 lime, juiced (can substitute lemon if lime is not available)
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat up oil in a large pot, add diced onion, garlic, and ginger. Cook on medium-low heat, stir frequently until the onion starts to appear translucent and smell aromatic, about 4-5 mins. 
  2. Stir in the jalapeño, garam masala, turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook for 1 min until it becomes fragrant. 
  3. Add chickpeas, tomato can, and coconut milk. Gentle simmer uncovered, until the chickpeas are soft, about 18-20 mins. 
  4. Turn off the heat completely and right before serving stir in lime juice and fresh cilantro.
  5. (Optional) Serve with rice cooked to preference, extra cilantro, yogurt, or naan.
